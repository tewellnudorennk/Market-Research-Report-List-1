<p><p>The air-fuel ratio sensor market is segmented into three primary types: zirconia oxygen sensors, titanium oxygen sensors, and narrow-band sensors. Zirconia sensors utilize zirconium dioxide to measure oxygen levels in exhaust gases, providing precise air-fuel ratio feedback. Titanium sensors, employing titanium dioxide, offer rapid response times, enhancing engine efficiency. Narrow-band sensors, which produce a voltage output within a specific range, are commonly used in emissions control to optimize fuel combustion. Each type plays a crucial role in improving automotive performance and emissions compliance.</p></p>
